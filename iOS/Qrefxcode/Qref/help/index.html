Getting Started

No checklist yet?

No problem. Qref checklists are but a tap away. Purchase and download the checklist you need directly from the app. Tap Download to view a list of available aircraft models. This list is being added to regularly, so check back if you don't see the checklist you need.

I purchased a checklist, but don't see it in the dashboard.

Sometimes, if an in-app purchase doesn't go through for some reason, simply download the checklist again. Both we and Apple keep track of your purchases. If you weren't charged for it the first time, you will be prompted to buy it. If you were charged, you'll be able to download it again at no additional cost. This is also the case if you remove your checklist from the dashboard.

Can I edit a checklist?

Absolutely! From the checklist, tap the pencil icon at the top of the page to turn on/off edit mode.

Qref Account

Why do I need a Qref account?

Setting up a Qref account allows for automatic backup of your changes and allows syncing across devices.

Please note that your Qref Checklist app account is separate from any other account, including a qref.com account. At this time, you can only access your checklists from an app.

Editing

How do I edit the aircraft tail number on the dashboard?

On the dashboard, tap the pencil icon at the top of the page to turn on edit mode. Tap on the desired aircraft and you will be prompted for the tail number.

The tail number will be synced with your account the next time you log in or select Manual Sync from the menu. This means you'll see the tail number (and any checklist edits) on any device you log in with using the Qref app.

How do I remove a checklist from the Dashboard?

On the dashboard, tap the pencil icon at the top of the page to turn on edit mode. Swipe the checklist you'd like to remove, then tap "Delete". If you want to add this checklist back, you can by simply downloading it again. You will not be charged for it again. Promise! One thing to keep in mind, though... your edits will be gone if you remove the checklist from the dashboard.

How do I reset a checklist to the original download?

If you have edited a checklist and want to revert back to the original version, simply remove the checklist from the Dashboard (see above), go to Downloads, and download the checklist again. Because we keep track of your purchases, you won't be charged for it again.

Syncing

Syncing between two or more devices

Syncing your edited checklists between multiple devices is easy. Your Qref account links your app to a cloud server that maintains a backup of all of your checklists, including any edits.

Syncing can be done 2 ways.

  1. Immediately after editing: When you are done editing on the first device, tap the pencil icon at the top of the page to turn off edit mode. Tap "Sync to Server" to send the changes you made to the Qref cloud server. There, they will be available for another device to sync to.
  2. Manually: At any time, you can manually sync your checklists and any edits made to the cloud server. To do this, tap the menu icon , then tap "Manual Sync" . If the checklist on your device is newer than on the server, it is sent to the server. Otherwise, if the checklist on the server is newer than your local version, the local version is replaced with server version.

Once the changes are sync'd, you can update any other devices by performing a manual sync as described above. The newer checklist on the server will replace the older checklist on your other devices.

Note: in order to sync across multiple devices, all devices must be signed in to the same account.

Multiple, Duplicate Checklists

Can I dupliacte a checklist?

Yes, and easily so. If you would like two different versions of the same checklist, simply download the checklist again. Once you have purchased a checklist, you will not be charged for another download. Tap "Download" at the bottom of the Dashboard and find the model you've already purchased. Tap on it, and then tap "Install". This will install another version of the checklist which you can access from the Dashboard. A new, random tail number will be assigned so you can tell the two apart.

Note: The new, second checklist will contain the default checklist data and will not include any edits made to the first version.
